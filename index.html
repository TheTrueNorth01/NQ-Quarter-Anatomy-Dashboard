📚 What is Quarter Anatomy?

Quarter Anatomy is a powerful trading framework that divides each candle into four equal time periods (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4) to understand where price extremes form and how they behave. This helps traders identify high-probability setups and optimal entry/exit points.

🎯 The Core Concept

Each trading candle (15min, 1h, 4h, etc.) is split into 4 equal quarters. We track:
• Where the HIGH forms (for bearish candles)
• Where the LOW forms (for bullish candles)
• Whether it holds until the candle closes

How a Candle is Divided

Q1
0-25% of time
First quarter
Q2
25-50% of time
Second quarter
Q3
50-75% of time
Third quarter
Q4
75-100% of time
Final quarter

For a 1-hour candle: Q1 = 0-15min, Q2 = 15-30min, Q3 = 30-45min, Q4 = 45-60min

⭐ The Key Metric: Q1 Hold Rate

Q1 Hold Rate = The probability that once Q1 sets the extreme (high for bears, low for bulls), it is NEVER revisited before the candle closes. A high Q1 hold rate (70%+) means Q1 is a reliable anchor point for trades.

🎯 How to Trade This Pattern

Step-by-step trading strategy using Q1 anchor

1
Wait for Q1 to Close
Let the first quarter of the candle complete to see where the extreme forms
2
Identify the Anchor
Bull: Q1 low. Bear: Q1 high. This becomes your key level
3
Place Your Stop
Just beyond Q1 extreme (70% chance it won't break)
4
Target Q4
Opposite extreme typically forms in Q4 (40-50% of time)
💡 Pro Tip: The Q1→Q4 Fade
When Q1 sets the extreme, there's a 40-50% probability the opposite extreme forms in Q4. This creates a "Q1→Q4 fade" setup: enter after Q1 close, target Q4 opposite extreme, stop just beyond Q1.

📊 Understanding Range vs Drift
Range/ATR: Total price oscillation in Q2-Q4 (normalized by ATR). Higher = more volatility.
Drift/ATR: Net directional movement in Q2-Q4. Near zero = price chops without trend after Q1.

Near-zero drift with non-trivial range confirms Q1 is the structural phase, Q2-Q4 are resolution/chop phases.
